Want to level up your or your team’s front-end development skills, but are overwhelmed by the sheer amount of resources on different topics and don’t have enough time to read the never-ending amount of articles & books available? Want to get up and running fast, learning the most recent and relevant skills to get the job done? I can help.

I am currently running front-end development workshops focusing on CSS and SVG. If your company, team, or event could benefit from a full or half-day workshop on one of these topics, I’d love to help provide you with one (or even two!).

The workshops are packed with practical examples, exercises and insights that attendees can take away and start applying right away. Whether you’re a designer, developer, or a company employing teams of both—there’s something for everyone.

I also run private workshops, so if you’re a company who wants to give its employees and extra, intense shot of front end, I’m here to help. You can also request multiple workshops spread over multiple days—from general front end design and development, to deep dives. Just let me know what you require and we can adjust accordingly.

The Workshops

  • The Front-End Espresso Shot

    An intense (as intense as an Espresso shot!), very technical full-day workshop covering a vast range of front-end topics from SVG to CSS: layout techniques with Grid and Flexbox, blend modes, shapes, custom properties a.k.a CSS variables, native feature detection, and responsive typography techniques, building a progressively enhanced and accessible front-end foundation from the ground up.

    Learn more about the workshop

  • The SVG Coffee Cup

    Like a cup of coffee on a busy morning, this workshop will kickstart & rocket up your SVG skillset and get you up and running using this powerful image (and document) format in your responsive Web design workflow. Using SVG, you can create accessible, semantic images, icons, and animated documents. In this workshop, we'll go over everything you need to know to start using SVG today: design, export and optimization, implementation (icon systems, images, and interactive documents) and animation.

    Learn more about the workshop
